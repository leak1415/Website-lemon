Samlor Korko

A hearty Khmer soup made with fish, vegetables, and roasted rice powder, often called the “stirring soup.”

Ingredients

  • 300g fish (catfish or snakehead)
  • 2 tbsp roasted rice powder
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (eggplant, green papaya, long beans)
  • 1 tbsp prahok (fermented fish paste)
  • 2 tbsp kroeung (lemongrass paste)

Steps

  1. Prepare kroeung paste and roast rice until golden, then grind.
  2. Boil water, add kroeung and prahok.
  3. Add vegetables and cook until tender.
  4. Add fish and roasted rice powder, simmer until thick.
  5. Serve hot with rice.

Benefits

Rich in protein, fiber, and antioxidants from local vegetables and fish. Roasted rice adds a nutty taste and energy.

Serving & Tips

  • Best served with steamed jasmine rice.
  • Use more vegetables for a heartier meal.

Samlor Machu Trey

A sweet-sour fish soup with pineapple, tomatoes, tamarind, and fresh herbs.

Ingredients

  • 300g fish fillet
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tamarind paste
  • Fresh herbs (sawtooth coriander, basil)

Steps

  1. Boil water, add tamarind paste.
  2. Add fish and cook gently.
  3. Add pineapple and tomatoes, simmer until tender.
  4. Season with fish sauce and sugar.
  5. Garnish with herbs before serving.

Benefits

Light, refreshing, and rich in vitamin C from pineapple and tomatoes. Helps with digestion and hydration.

Serving & Tips

  • Serve hot with jasmine rice.
  • Add chili for a spicy kick.

Samlor Machu Kreung

A sour soup with lemongrass kroeung paste, fish, and vegetables.

Ingredients

  • 300g fish
  • 2 tbsp lemongrass kroeung paste
  • 2 tbsp tamarind juice
  • 1 cup green papaya or long beans
  • Fresh herbs (kaffir lime leaves, basil)

Steps

  1. Bring water to boil, add kroeung paste.
  2. Add tamarind juice and fish, cook until tender.
  3. Add vegetables and simmer until done.
  4. Season with fish sauce and palm sugar.
  5. Top with herbs before serving.

Benefits

Sour and aromatic, aids digestion and boosts immunity with lemongrass and herbs.

Serving & Tips

  • Enjoy with hot rice or rice noodles.
  • Adjust sourness by adding more tamarind.

Samlor Kari Sach Moan

A chicken curry soup with coconut milk, sweet potatoes, and lemongrass paste.

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 2 sweet potatoes (cubed)
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp lemongrass paste
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • Fish sauce, salt, and sugar to taste

Steps

  1. Sauté garlic, onion, and lemongrass paste until fragrant.
  2. Add chicken and stir until slightly browned.
  3. Pour in coconut milk, turmeric, and sweet potatoes.
  4. Simmer until chicken is tender and potatoes are soft.
  5. Season with fish sauce, salt, and sugar. Serve hot.

Benefits

Rich in protein and healthy fats, this curry soup provides energy and immune-boosting nutrients from turmeric.

Serving & Tips

Best enjoyed with steamed rice or French baguette. Add chili for extra heat if desired.

Samlor Chap Chhay

A light vegetable stew made with assorted greens, mushrooms, and tofu or pork.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cabbage (shredded)
  • 1 cup spinach
  • 100g mushrooms (sliced)
  • 150g pork belly or tofu
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • Salt and fish sauce to taste

Steps

  1. Sauté garlic and pork (or tofu) until lightly cooked.
  2. Add vegetables and mushrooms, stir for 2 minutes.
  3. Add water and simmer until vegetables are tender.
  4. Season to taste. Serve warm.

Benefits

This soup is high in fiber and antioxidants, promoting digestion and overall health.

Serving & Tips

Eat with rice or noodles. Add more vegetables for variety.
