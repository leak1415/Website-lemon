Street food & Snacks

Baguette Sandwich (Num Pang)

A fresh baguette filled with meats, pickled vegetables, herbs, and chili sauce, perfect for a quick street meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 baguette
  • Grilled pork or chicken
  • Pickled carrots and daikon
  • Cucumber slices
  • Fresh cilantro and chili sauce

Steps

  1. Slice the baguette open and lightly toast it if desired.
  2. Layer with grilled meat, pickled vegetables, cucumber, and herbs.
  3. Drizzle chili sauce over filling.
  4. Serve immediately as a street snack.

Benefits

Quick, flavorful, and provides a balanced combination of carbs, protein, and veggies.

Serving & Tips

  • Best eaten fresh and warm.
  • Adjust chili sauce to taste for spiciness.

Nom Banh Chok

Cambodian rice noodle dish topped with fish gravy, fresh herbs, and vegetables. A popular breakfast street food.

Ingredients

  • Rice noodles
  • Fish gravy or curry
  • Bean sprouts, cucumbers, and fresh herbs
  • Lime and chili for garnish

Steps

  1. Boil rice noodles until soft and drain.
  2. Pour fish gravy over noodles.
  3. Top with fresh vegetables and herbs.
  4. Add lime and chili to taste before serving.

Benefits

Light and refreshing, rich in protein from fish and fiber from vegetables.

Serving & Tips

  • Serve immediately for best freshness.
  • Customize spice level with chili.

Bai Sach Chrouk

Grilled pork marinated in garlic and coconut milk, served with rice and pickled vegetables.

Ingredients

  • Pork slices
  • Garlic and coconut milk marinade
  • Steamed rice
  • Pickled vegetables

Steps

  1. Marinate pork in garlic and coconut milk for several hours.
  2. Grill pork until fully cooked and slightly charred.
  3. Serve with steamed rice and pickled vegetables.

Benefits

Rich in protein and flavor, a classic Cambodian street meal.

Serving & Tips

  • Eat while hot for the best taste.
  • Optional: add a side of broth for a complete meal.

Ang Dtray-Meuk

Fresh squid grilled over charcoal, often brushed with a savory sauce and enjoyed hot.

Ingredients

  • Fresh squid
  • Savory marinade (soy sauce, garlic, sugar)
  • Lime and chili for serving

Steps

  1. Clean squid and marinate in sauce for 30 minutes.
  2. Grill over medium heat until cooked through.
  3. Serve hot with lime and chili dipping sauce.

Benefits

High in protein, low in fat, and full of umami flavor.

Serving & Tips

  • Serve immediately to enjoy tender texture.
  • Perfect as a snack or part of a street meal.

Fried Insects

A crunchy street snack of locally sourced insects, seasoned and deep-fried, popular in Cambodian markets.

Ingredients

  • Edible insects (crickets, grasshoppers, or silkworms)
  • Oil for frying
  • Salt, chili, or seasoning powder

Steps

  1. Clean and prepare insects.
  2. Deep-fry in hot oil until crispy.
  3. Season with salt, chili, or spices.
  4. Serve immediately.

Benefits

High in protein and a crunchy alternative snack, enjoyed as a novelty or traditional delicacy.

Serving & Tips

  • Serve hot for best crunch.
  • Pair with beer or soft drinks for street-style enjoyment.

ចេកចៀន (Chek Chiean)

Ripe bananas coated in a light batter and deep-fried until golden and crispy. A sweet and popular Cambodian street snack.

Ingredients

  • Ripe bananas
  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Oil for deep frying

Steps

  1. Slice bananas into halves or thirds.
  2. Mix rice flour, sugar, and water to make a smooth batter.
  3. Dip banana slices into the batter, coating evenly.
  4. Deep-fry in hot oil until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Drain excess oil and serve warm.

Benefits

Provides natural sweetness, energy from bananas, and a tasty crispy snack.

Serving & Tips

  • Serve hot for maximum crispiness.
  • Optional: sprinkle with sugar or drizzle with coconut syrup.
