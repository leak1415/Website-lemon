Fish Amok

A traditional Cambodian steamed fish curry with coconut
milk and kroeung, served in banana leaves.

Ingredients

  • 500g fish fillet (catfish, snakehead, or tilapia)
  • 200ml coconut milk
  • 1 cup kroeung (Cambodian curry paste)
  • 2 eggs
  • Banana leaves or bowls for steaming
  • Fresh kaffir lime leaves

Steps

  1. Prepare kroeung paste (or use ready-made).
  2. Mix fish with kroeung, coconut milk, and beaten eggs.
  3. Line banana leaves into small bowls and pour the mixture.
  4. Steam for 20–25 minutes until set.
  5. Garnish with lime leaves and serve hot.

Samlor Machu Trey

A refreshing sour fish soup flavored with tamarind, lemongrass, and fresh herbs.

Ingredients

  • 300g fish (snakehead, tilapia, or catfish)
  • 2 tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, bruised
  • 2 tbsp tamarind paste or juice
  • Fresh herbs: basil, sawtooth coriander
  • Salt, sugar, fish sauce

Steps

  1. Bring water to a boil with lemongrass.
  2. Add tamarind, tomatoes, and fish.
  3. Season with salt, sugar, and fish sauce.
  4. Simmer until fish is cooked through.
  5. Add fresh herbs just before serving.

Prahok Khtis

A rich dish of fermented fish paste cooked with coconut milk, minced pork, and herbs.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp prahok (fermented fish paste)
  • 200g minced pork
  • 150ml coconut milk
  • 2 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar
  • Fresh chili and herbs

Steps

  1. Fry garlic until fragrant.
  2. Add minced pork and cook until browned.
  3. Stir in prahok and coconut milk.
  4. Simmer until thick and flavorful.
  5. Serve with fresh vegetables and rice.

Grilled Fish with Herbs

Whole fish grilled and stuffed with lemongrass, kaffir lime leaves, and galangal.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole fish (about 1kg)
  • 2 stalks lemongrass, chopped
  • 3 kaffir lime leaves
  • 2 slices galangal
  • Fish sauce, salt, pepper

Steps

  1. Clean and score the fish.
  2. Stuff cavity with lemongrass, galangal, and lime leaves.
  3. Rub fish with salt, pepper, and fish sauce.
  4. Grill over charcoal until golden and cooked through.
  5. Serve with dipping sauce.

Ginger Fried Fish

Fish stir-fried with ginger, garlic, and spring onions.

Ingredients

  • 400g fish fillet
  • 2 tbsp ginger, julienned
  • 2 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 2 spring onions, chopped
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • Cooking oil

Steps

  1. Fry fish pieces until golden, set aside.
  2. Sauté garlic and ginger in oil.
  3. Add soy sauce and spring onions.
  4. Toss fish back into the wok, stir to combine.
  5. Serve hot with rice.

Grilled Shrimp

Fresh shrimp marinated with garlic, lemongrass, and spices, then grilled to perfection.

Ingredients

  • 500g fresh shrimp
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, minced
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • Chili flakes (optional)

Steps

  1. Marinate shrimp with garlic, lemongrass, fish sauce, and oil.
  2. Thread onto skewers if desired.
  3. Grill over charcoal until pink and slightly charred.
  4. Serve with dipping sauce.

Steamed Fish

Steamed fish with herbs, vegetables, and dipping sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole fish (about 800g)
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • Ginger slices
  • Spring onions
  • Soy sauce

Steps

  1. Clean fish and place on steaming plate.
  2. Add garlic, ginger, and spring onions.
  3. Steam for 15–20 minutes until fish is cooked.
  4. Pour soy sauce over before serving.

ស្ងោរជ្រក់ (Samlor Sngor Nga Trey)

Fish soup with fingerroot, lemongrass, and holy basil.

Ingredients

  • 300g fish
  • 2 stalks lemongrass
  • Fingerroot, sliced
  • 1 tomato
  • Holy basil
  • Fish sauce, salt

Steps

  1. Bring water to boil with lemongrass and fingerroot.
  2. Add fish and tomato.
  3. Season with fish sauce and salt.
  4. Finish with holy basil leaves before serving.

ត្រីបំពងម្សៅ (Crispy Fried Fish)

Crispy fried fish coated in seasoned flour.

Ingredients

  • 500g fish fillet
  • 1 cup flour
  • Salt, pepper
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  1. Season flour with salt and pepper.
  2. Coat fish pieces in flour mixture.
  3. Deep fry until golden brown and crispy.
  4. Drain on paper towels and serve.

ត្រីបំពងខ្ទិះ (Fried Fish with Coconut Curry)

Fish fried and topped with creamy coconut curry sauce.

Ingredients

  • 500g fish fillet
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp curry paste
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  1. Fry fish fillets until golden.
  2. In a pan, simmer coconut milk with curry paste and fish sauce.
  3. Pour sauce over fried fish.
  4. Serve with rice.

ខ្យងអាំង (Grilled Lobster)

Grilled lobster with herbs and spices, served hot.

Ingredients

  • 2 whole lobsters
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • Fresh herbs (parsley, coriander)
  • Salt, pepper

Steps

  1. Split lobster in half.
  2. Brush with garlic butter and seasonings.
  3. Grill until meat is opaque and slightly charred.
  4. Garnish with herbs and serve immediately.

ទឹកគ្រឿង (Tuk Krueng)

A flavorful Cambodian spice sauce, perfect for dipping or drizzling over seafood.

Ingredients

  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 2 red chilies
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar

Steps

  1. Pound garlic and chilies into paste.
  2. Add fish sauce, lime juice, and sugar.
  3. Mix until sugar dissolves.
  4. Serve as a dipping sauce.

ប្រហុកអាំង (Grilled Prahok)

Grilled prahok paste wrapped in banana leaves.

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp prahok
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • Chili peppers
  • Banana leaves

Steps

  1. Mix prahok with garlic and chilies.
  2. Wrap mixture in banana leaves.
  3. Grill until fragrant and smoky.
  4. Serve with fresh vegetables.

ប្រហុកចំហុយ (Steamed Prahok)

Steamed fermented fish with garlic and chili.

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp prahok
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • Fresh chilies
  • Banana leaf or steaming plate

Steps

  1. Mix prahok with garlic and chilies.
  2. Place mixture in banana leaf or bowl.
  3. Steam for 15 minutes until cooked through.
  4. Serve hot with rice.

ប្រហុកខ្ទឹមស (Prahok with Garlic)

Fermented fish dip with garlic and chili peppers.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp prahok
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • Fresh chilies
  • 1 tbsp oil

Steps

  1. Heat oil in pan, fry garlic and chilies.
  2. Add prahok and stir-fry until fragrant.
  3. Cook until mixture is slightly thickened.
  4. Serve with rice and vegetables.
