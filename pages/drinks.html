Drink

ទឹក​អំពៅ (Teuk Ambok)

Fermented rice drink with a slightly sweet and tangy flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked sticky rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tbsp sugar

Steps

  1. Mix cooked sticky rice with water and sugar.
  2. Leave to ferment for 1-2 days at room temperature.
  3. Strain liquid and serve chilled.

Benefits

Contains probiotics, aids digestion, and provides mild natural energy.

Serving & Tips

  • Serve chilled or over ice.
  • Do not ferment for too long to avoid strong sourness.

ទឹក​ខ្ញី (Teuk Mreah)

Ginger and lemongrass herbal drink, often consumed warm for health benefits.

Ingredients

  • 1-inch piece of ginger
  • 3 stalks lemongrass
  • 4 cups water
  • Honey to taste

Steps

  1. Crush ginger and lemongrass.
  2. Boil in water for 10-15 minutes.
  3. Strain and add honey.
  4. Serve warm.

Benefits

Boosts immunity, aids digestion, and relieves cold symptoms.

Serving & Tips

  • Serve warm for best effect.
  • Can add lime slices for extra zest.

ទឹក​មៀន (Teuk Mieng Khai)

A traditional herbal tea made from lemongrass and other herbs.

Ingredients

  • 5 stalks lemongrass
  • 4 cups water
  • 1-2 tbsp honey

Steps

  1. Crush lemongrass stalks and boil in water for 10 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea into cups.
  3. Add honey to taste and serve warm or chilled.

Benefits

Calming, aids digestion, and rich in antioxidants.

Serving & Tips

  • Serve warm to soothe throat and stomach.
  • Can add a slice of lime for extra flavor.

ទឹកអំពិលទុំ (Teuk Ampil Tum)

Sweet and slightly salty palm drink, traditionally served during festivals.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup palm sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • Pinch of salt
  • Ice cubes (optional)

Steps

  1. Boil water with palm sugar and salt until sugar dissolves.
  2. Strain if needed and cool.
  3. Serve chilled or with ice.

Benefits

Refreshing, provides energy, and a natural sweet-salty balance.

Serving & Tips

  • Serve cold for maximum refreshment.
  • Garnish with mint leaves if desired.

ទឹកតែជីអង្កាម (Teuk Chi Angkam)

Traditional fermented sugarcane drink, lightly sweet and refreshing.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sugarcane juice
  • 2 cups water
  • Ice cubes

Steps

  1. Mix sugarcane juice with water.
  2. Chill in refrigerator or add ice cubes.
  3. Serve cold.

Benefits

Refreshing, natural sweetness, provides hydration and mild energy.

Serving & Tips

  • Serve cold for the best experience.
  • Can garnish with sugarcane sticks.

កាហ្វេរតនគិរី (Coffee Rotanakiri)

Rich Cambodian coffee made from local Rotanakiri beans, served hot or iced.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp ground Rotanakiri coffee
  • 1 cup hot water
  • Condensed milk or sugar to taste

Steps

  1. Brew coffee using hot water.
  2. Add sugar or condensed milk to taste.
  3. Serve hot or over ice.

Benefits

Provides energy, rich aroma, and a unique Cambodian coffee flavor.

Serving & Tips

  • Serve iced with sweetened condensed milk for a popular Cambodian style.
