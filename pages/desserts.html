Desserts

Num Ansom Chek

A traditional sticky rice cake filled with banana and coconut, wrapped in banana leaves.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups glutinous rice
  • 2 ripe bananas
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • Banana leaves for wrapping
  • Pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Soak glutinous rice for 3 hours, then drain.
  2. Mash bananas and mix with coconut, sugar, and salt.
  3. Place rice and banana mixture on banana leaf, fold into a square packet.
  4. Steam for 1 hour until rice is cooked.
  5. Cool slightly and serve.

Benefits

Provides energy from sticky rice and natural sweetness from bananas and coconut.

Serving & Tips

  • Serve warm or at room temperature.
  • Can add sesame seeds for extra flavor.

Chek Ktis

Sweet coconut milk dessert with ripe banana, often served during Khmer festivals.

Ingredients

  • 3 ripe bananas
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp roasted sesame seeds

Steps

  1. Slice bananas and place in a saucepan.
  2. Add coconut milk, sugar, and salt, cook over medium heat until bananas are soft.
  3. Pour into serving bowls and sprinkle sesame seeds on top.
  4. Serve warm.

Benefits

Rich in potassium, healthy fats, and natural sugars for quick energy.

Serving & Tips

  • Can serve chilled for a refreshing summer dessert.
  • Add a dash of vanilla for extra aroma.

Num Plae Ai

Steamed sticky rice dumplings filled with coconut and mung bean paste, wrapped in banana leaves.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups glutinous rice
  • 1/2 cup mung bean paste
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • Banana leaves for wrapping
  • Pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Soak glutinous rice for 2–3 hours, then drain.
  2. Mix mung bean paste with coconut and sugar.
  3. Wrap a spoon of rice and filling in banana leaf, seal packet.
  4. Steam for 45 minutes until fully cooked.
  5. Cool and serve.

Benefits

Good source of energy and protein from mung beans, plus dietary fiber.

Serving & Tips

  • Serve warm for better flavor.
  • Use pandan leaves for added fragrance.

អាកោរត្នោត (Akaw Tnot)

Sweet sticky rice balls with palm sugar, wrapped in banana leaves, often served at festivals.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups glutinous rice
  • 1/2 cup palm sugar
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • Banana leaves for wrapping
  • Pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Soak glutinous rice for 2–3 hours, then drain.
  2. Mix rice with a pinch of salt and form into small balls.
  3. Place palm sugar and coconut inside rice balls, wrap with banana leaves.
  4. Steam for 45 minutes until cooked.
  5. Cool slightly and serve.

Benefits

High in energy and provides fiber from coconut; a natural sweet treat.

Serving & Tips

  • Serve warm for best taste.
  • Can sprinkle toasted sesame seeds for added flavor.

ក្រឡាន (Krolan)

Sticky rice with coconut and beans, stuffed inside bamboo tubes and roasted over charcoal.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups glutinous rice
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 1/4 cup black beans (soaked overnight)
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • Bamboo tubes

Steps

  1. Mix glutinous rice, coconut, sugar, and soaked beans.
  2. Stuff mixture into bamboo tubes and seal the ends.
  3. Roast bamboo over charcoal, turning occasionally for even cooking.
  4. Once cooked, remove bamboo and allow to cool slightly.
  5. Slice and serve.

Benefits

Provides protein from beans and energy from sticky rice; naturally sweet and satisfying.

Serving & Tips

  • Serve warm for aromatic flavor.
  • Perfect for outdoor gatherings or festivals.

នំចាក់ចាន់ (Num Chak Chan)

A traditional Cambodian layered steamed cake made with rice flour, coconut milk, and palm sugar, known for its colorful layers and sweet flavor.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup palm sugar (melted)
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • Food coloring (optional, for layers)

Steps

  1. Mix rice flour, coconut milk, palm sugar, and salt until smooth.
  2. Divide batter into portions and add food coloring if desired.
  3. Grease a steaming tray and pour a thin layer of batter.
  4. Steam each layer for 5-7 minutes until set before adding the next layer.
  5. Repeat until all layers are steamed.
  6. Allow cake to cool, then cut into pieces and serve.

Benefits

Rich in energy and naturally sweet; coconut milk provides healthy fats, and rice flour is a gluten-free carbohydrate source.

Serving & Tips

  • Serve at room temperature or slightly chilled.
  • Use a variety of colors to make the layers more visually appealing.
  • Cut with a wet knife to prevent sticking.
