Discover Khmer Traditional Food

Explore the rich flavors of Cambodia with authentic recipes, cultural insights, and health benefits of each dish.

Main Contents

Fish & Seafood

Fish is central to Cambodian cuisine, especially from the Tonle Sap and Mekong. Popular dishes include Fish Amok, Samlor Machu Trey, Prahok, Trey Cha Kdam, and grilled fish with herbs.

Meat Dishes

Chicken, pork, beef, and duck are cooked with local spices. Highlights include Ginger Chicken, Chhar Kroeung Sach Ko, Num Banh Chok Sach Moan, roast duck with herbs, and pork skewers.

Soups & Stews

Soups (Samlor) are eaten daily. Popular ones include Samlor Korkor, Samlor Machu, Samlor Kari, and Babaw (rice porridge).

Noodle & Rice Dishes

Staples include Nom Banh Chok, Kuy Teav, Bai Sach Chrouk, and fried rice with crab or shrimp.

Street Food & Snacks

Found in markets: Num Pang, Lort Cha, crispy spring rolls, grilled skewers, balut, and edible insects.

Vegetables & Salads

Dishes include Bok Lahong, stir-fried morning glory, eggplant stir-fry with basil, and banana blossom salad.

Desserts & Sweets

Popular desserts include Num Ansom, Num Chak Kachan, Num Plae Ai, pumpkin custard, and banana fritters.

Drinks & Beverages

Refreshing options include sugarcane juice, palm wine, iced coffee, lemongrass tea, and coconut water.

Pickles and Seasonings

Enhance meals with Prahok, Kroeung (spice paste), fish sauce dip, and pickled vegetables.

Festive & Traditional Foods

Special dishes for ceremonies and festivals such as Num Ansom, Kralan, roast pig, and Khmer curry, often served at weddings and holidays.

Breakfast Dishes

Start the day with favorites like Bai Sach Chrouk (pork with rice), Kuy Teav (noodle soup), and Nom Banh Chok (rice noodle with curry).

Vegetarian & Vegan Dishes

Buddhist-inspired meals with no meat: vegetable stir-fries, tofu dishes, jackfruit curry, and coconut-based soups.
